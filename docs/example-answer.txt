Some diagnostic techniques that use Illumina sequencing for bacteria include:

- **Amplicon Sequencing**:
  - Universal bacterial, fungal, or parasitic rRNA sequencing from multiple body
fluids and tissues for multiplexed pathogen detection (available clinical test)
[1].
  - Multiplexed primer panels for targeted analyses from multiple body fluids
and tissues (not currently available as a clinical test) [1].

- **Metagenomic Sequencing**:
  - Blood (plasma) for culture-negative sepsis, endocarditis, febrile
neutropenia, fever of unknown origin, or monitoring of immunocompromised
patients (available clinical test) [1].
  - Respiratory secretions for culture-negative and/or PCR-negative pneumonia
(available clinical test) [1].
  - Cerebrospinal fluid for undiagnosed meningitis, encephalitis, or myelitis
(available clinical test) [1].
  - Infected tissue or other body fluids for culture-negative infections (not
currently available as a clinical test) [1].

- **Specific Study Example**:
  - A study utilized the Illumina Nextseq CN500 sequencer for metagenomic
sequencing of drainage fluid to diagnose postoperative intra-abdominal
infections, generating approximately 20 million reads per library [5].

These techniques leverage the high throughput and efficiency of Illumina
sequencing platforms for the detection and characterization of bacterial
pathogens in clinical samples.
